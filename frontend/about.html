about sybron

sybron is a healthcare data marketplace built on a permissioned blockchain. we believe patients should own and benefit from their health data.

the problem

right now, your health data is scattered across hospitals, labs, insurers, and pharmacies. you can't easily access it. you certainly don't profit from it. meanwhile, these organizations sell your data to pharma companies and researchers without your knowledge or consent.

our solution

sybron gives you control. you register through a hospital (for verification), but your data stays encrypted and under your control. when researchers want access, they come to you. you set the terms—how long they can access it, for what purpose, at what price. you can revoke access anytime.

and you get paid. directly. in HEALTH tokens. 95% goes to you, 5% to the platform.

the technology

sybron runs on hyperledger besu, a permissioned ethereum-compatible blockchain. this means:

  • only verified hospitals, research institutions, and regulators can run nodes
  • all transactions are transparent and auditable (for compliance)
  • no mining, no energy waste—uses QBFT consensus
  • smart contracts handle consent, payments, and access control automatically

who runs sybron?

the network is operated by a consortium of healthcare organizations. no single entity controls it. the sybron foundation provides the software and coordinates the network, but governance is shared.

faq

is my real identity visible on the blockchain?

no. only a pseudonymous ID (a hash) is stored on-chain. your real identity is verified off-chain by a hospital and never touches the blockchain.

where is my actual health data stored?

encrypted on IPFS (a distributed file system). the blockchain only stores references and access permissions, not the data itself.

what if I change my mind about sharing?

you can revoke consent at any time. the researcher will immediately lose access to your data. (they keep any data already downloaded, but can't access new data.)

how much can I earn?

depends on your data. rare conditions, complete genomics, or longitudinal data are more valuable. you set your own price, and researchers can offer more.

who can access my data?

only verified researchers from approved institutions. they go through background checks and must be approved by network validators before they can request any data.
